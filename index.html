Introduction To Percentage

Percentages

A percentage is a ratio that represents a part of a whole, out of 100. The % symbol stands for "per hundred."

For example, 50% means 50 out of 100, or half of a whole.

How to Calculate Percentages?

To find a percentage, use this formula:

Percentage = PartWhole × 100

Example: Finding a Percentage
You scored 21 out of 24 in a test. What percentage did you get?
Part = 21, Whole = 24

2124 × 100 = 0.875 × 100 = 87.5%

So, you got 87.5% on your test!

Example 2: Finding a Missing Part or Whole
If 45% of a class plays sports and there are 117 such students, how many students are in the class?

Percentage (%) = 45, Part = 117, Whole = ?
Using the formula:

Whole= PartPercentage × 100

Whole= 11745 × 100 = 2.6 × 100 = 260

So, there are 260 students in the class.
Example: Finding Complementary Percentages
Since a whole is 100%, we can find missing percentages by subtracting known values.

A bag has 25 marbles, and 56% of them are blue.
To find the red marbles:

100% − 56% = 44%

That is, 44% of 25 = 11

So, there are 11 red marbles.

How to Convert a Ratio to a Percentage?

To convert a ratio to a percentage, follow these steps:

  1. Express the ratio as a fraction.
  2. Convert the fraction to a decimal by dividing.
  3. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage and put the percent sign %.

Example 1: Convert 3:5 to Percentage

Step 1: Write as fraction = ⅗
Step 2: Convert to percentage: (⅗) × 100 = 0.6 × 100 = 60%.

Percentage of each part in a ratio

If a ratio is a:b, then the percentage of each part is:

aa + b × 100 and ba + b × 100

Example: Find the percentage of 3 in the ratio 3:2.

33 + 2 × 100 = 0.6 × 100 = 60%
